About me

I'm Mika Bradley, an MPhil Student in Linguistics at University College London.

I'm interested primarily in Formal Semantics, as well as its interfaces with Syntax and Pragmatics. I'm especially interested in the Syntax and Lexical Semantics of Countability, Number and Individuation.

Some of the languages I enjoy drawing data from are English, Japanese, German, Singlish and Malay.

I wrote my Bachelor's thesis Yasu Sudo on something I call 'quasi-countability', a phenomenon which I'd like to some day write an accessible introduction to. For now though, feel free to read my abstract.

Research Interests

Broad: Formal Semantics & Pragmatics, Logic, Computational Linguistics.

Narrow: Countability, Number & Individuation, Algebraic Semantics, Social Meaning, Bayesian Epistemology, Type Theory.
