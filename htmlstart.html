About me

I'm Mika Bradley, currently a final year undergraduate student in Experimental Linguistics at University College London.

I'm interested primarily in Formal Semantics, as well as its interfaces with Syntax and Pragmatics. I'm especially interested in the Syntax and Lexical Semantics of Countability, Number and Individuation.

Some of the languages I enjoy drawing data from are English, Japanese, German, Singlish and Malay.

I'm currently writing a thesis supervised by Yasu Sudo.

Research Interests

Broad: Formal Semantics & Pragmatics, Logic, Mathematical Psychology, Computational Linguistics.

Narrow: Countability, Degrees, Algebraic Semantics, Concept and Belief representation in Humans and Machines, Type Theory, Functional Programming, Honorification & Expressive Meaning, Japanese Linguistics.
